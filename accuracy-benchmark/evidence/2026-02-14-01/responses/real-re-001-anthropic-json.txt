## Summary Conclusions

1. **Extreme Concentration**: Top 30 properties represent $18.3B in assessed value
2. **Development Intensity**: Modern supertalls (One Vanderbilt, Hudson Yards) achieve FAR ratios 3-4x base zoning through bonuses
3. **Two-Tier Market**: Luxury residential ($3.5-4.8M/unit) vs. affordable ($67-90K/unit) 
4. **Tax Policy Impact**: 39.4% of value is tax-exempt, concentrated in public facilities, government projects, and affordable housing
5. **Land Use**: 77% are pure commercial office towers, reflecting Manhattan's role as a business center
6. **Historical Range**: Buildings span 1930-2019, with newest towers (2015-2019) achieving highest density

This dataset represents Manhattan's most valuable real estate, dominated by supertall office towers, public infrastructure, and a small number of ultra-luxury residential properties.
