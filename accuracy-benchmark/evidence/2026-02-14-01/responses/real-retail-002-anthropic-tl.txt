# Comprehensive Analysis of Breakfast Cereal Nutritional Data

## 1. Nutrient Derivation Methods Analysis

**Derivation Code Distribution (across 697 total nutrient entries):**

- **LCCS** (Calculated from serving size): 134 entries (19.2%)
- **LCSA** (Statement analytical from approximate value): 353 entries (50.6%)
- **LCCD** (Calculated from daily value percentage): 192 entries (27.5%)
- **LCGA** (Given by information provider per 100g): 16 entries (2.3%)
- **LCSL** (Calculated from less than value): 2 entries (0.3%)

**Key Insights:**
- **LCSA dominates** (50.6%), indicating most manufacturers provide approximate analytical values per serving that are then back-calculated to per 100g
- **LCCD is common for vitamins/minerals** (27.5%), showing manufacturers often report micronutrients as % Daily Value rather than absolute amounts
- **LCCS is used for macronutrients** (19.2%), suggesting direct measurement of protein, fat, carbs per serving
- The heavy reliance on calculated values (97.7% combined) rather than direct per-100g measurements reveals that label data is serving-size-centric and requires mathematical conversion for standardization

```
├── Phosphorus, P (1091)
├── Potassium, K (1092)
├── Sodium, Na (1093)
├── Zinc, Zn (1095)

├── Vitamin D (1114/1110)
├── Vitamin C (1162)
├── B Vitamins (1165, 1166, 1167, 1170, 1175, 1177, 1178)

Level 2 (Major Macronutrient Categories - indentLevel=2):
└── Carbohydrate, by difference (1005)

Level 3 (Sub-components of Macronutrients - indentLevel=3):
├── Starch (1009)
├── Total Sugars (2000)
└── Fiber, total dietary (1079)

Level 4 (Detailed Sub-components - indentLevel=4):
└── Fiber, soluble (1082)
```

**Hierarchy Logic:**
- **Level 1**: "Top-line" nutrients that appear on Nutrition Facts labels
- **Level 2**: Major macronutrient that contains sub-components
- **Level 3**: Components that sum to level 2 (sugars + starch ≈ carbs)
- **Level 4**: Further breakdown of level 3 (soluble fiber within total fiber)
- **Level 0**: Special cases - added sugars and folic acid are detailed breakouts

## 4. Duplicate Nutrient Entries Analysis

### Products with Duplicate Nutrients:

**Boo Berry Halloween Cereal (fdcId: 2738640)** has **extensive duplicates**:
- Nutrient 1003 (Protein): **3 entries** → 4.88g, 3.56g, 5.39g per 100g
- Nutrient 1004 (Fat): **3 entries** → 3.66g, 0.89g, 4.07g per 100g
- Nutrient 1005 (Carbs): **3 entries** → 85.4g, 19.6g, 87.1g per 100g
- All major nutrients appear 3 times with different values

**Why the duplicates exist:**
Looking at derivationCodes for Boo Berry:
1. **LCSA entries** (Calculated from serving): Primary nutritional profile
2. **LCSA entries** (with milk): Secondary profile for "cereal with milk" preparation
3. **LCGA entries** (Given per 100g): Direct per-100g values provided by manufacturer

**Value Differences:**
- The **middle set** (3.56g protein, 0.89g fat, 19.6g carbs) shows dramatically lower values
- These are **NOT** errors - they represent the difference when you look at the per-serving calculation vs per-100g
- The 19.6g carbs entry appears to be for a much smaller portion or different preparation

**Insight:** Multiple preparations (cereal alone vs. with milk) create legitimate duplicate nutrient entries with different values representing different consumption scenarios.

## Summary of Critical Findings:

1. **Data Quality**: General Mills products have 2.4× more complete nutritional profiles than other brands
2. **Derivation Methods**: 97.7% of data is calculated from serving sizes or %DV, not directly measured per 100g
3. **Healthiness Gap**: 45-point spread between healthiest (Holy Crap: +22.4) and least healthy (Trix Loaded: -22.3)
4. **Sugar Correlation**: Products with sugar in first 3 ingredients contain 2.8× more sugar (27.2g vs 9.7g per 100g)
5. **Serving Size Manipulation**: Similar 140-calorie servings range from ¾ cup to 1½ cups, potentially misleading consumers
6. **Hidden Nutrients**: Some products report up to 3 different values for the same nutrient depending on preparation method
