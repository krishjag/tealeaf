## 4. Urban vs Rural Comparison: King County vs Ferry County

| Measure | King (Urban) | Ferry (Rural) | Difference |
|---------|--------------|---------------|------------|
| **Smoking (CSMOKING)** | 7.2% | 16.0% | +8.8 pts (122% higher) |
| **Obesity (OBESITY)** | 22.6% | 40.2% | +17.6 pts (78% higher) |
| **Depression (DEPRESSION)** | 23.5% | 25.0% | +1.5 pts (6% higher) |
| **Lack of Insurance (ACCESS2)** | 6.8% | 8.5% | +1.7 pts (25% higher) |

**Key Finding:** Ferry County shows substantially worse outcomes in smoking and obesity, but only modest differences in depression and insurance access compared to King County.

## 5. Widest Health Risk Behavior Disparity

**Physical Inactivity (LPA)** shows the widest disparity:
- **Highest:** Yakima County - 27.9% (24.2-31.7)
- **Lowest:** King County - 13.9% (12.1-15.8)
- **Disparity:** 14.0 percentage points (101% difference)

Other RISKBEH disparities:
- Smoking: 8.8 percentage points (Ferry 16.0% vs King 7.2%)
- Sleep: 7.9 percentage points (Pierce 37.0% vs Whatcom 29.1%)
- Binge Drinking: 3.7 percentage points (Whatcom 17.9% vs Island 14.2%)

## 6. Counties Significantly Above Average (5+ percentage points on 3+ measures)

**Only Ferry County** meets this threshold, exceeding the 10-county average by 5+ percentage points on **4 health outcomes**:

| Measure | Ferry Rate | 10-County Avg | Difference |
|---------|------------|---------------|------------|
| Arthritis | 38.0% | 26.8% | +11.2 pts |
| High Blood Pressure | 41.1% | 32.5% | +8.6 pts |
| High Cholesterol | 41.2% | 35.8% | +5.4 pts |
| Obesity | 40.2% | 33.4% | +6.8 pts |

**Notable:** Yakima County exceeds by 5+ points on 2 measures (Obesity +6.3 pts, All Teeth Lost +6.5 pts) but does not reach the 3+ threshold.

---

### Summary Insights:
- **Rural-urban divide is stark:** Ferry County (rural, pop. 7,497) shows consistently worse health outcomes across multiple measures
- **King County advantage:** Most urban county shows best outcomes in obesity, diabetes, smoking, and physical activity
- **Physical inactivity** is the most geographically disparate risk behavior
- **High cholesterol** is the most prevalent health outcome statewide, appearing in top 3 for all 10 counties
