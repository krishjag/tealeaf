## 5. Widest Disparity in Health Risk Behaviors

- **Highest:** Yakima County at 27.9% (CI: 24.2-31.7)

- **Disparity:** 14.0 percentage points

**Other Risk Behavior Disparities:**

---

### Summary Insights

The data reveals significant health disparities between urban and rural Washington counties. **Ferry County** (population 7,497) shows the most concerning health profile with four major outcomes exceeding state averages by substantial margins. **King County** (population 2.2M) demonstrates the healthiest outcomes across nearly all measures. The urban-rural divide is particularly stark for obesity, diabetes, and physical inactivity, suggesting systemic differences in access to healthy food, healthcare, and recreational infrastructure.
