### Near or over zoning limit (highest utilization)
Many of these lots show **builtfar ≈ allowed FAR** (often exactly 100%) because the max FAR field equals the district cap used in PLUTO:

Examples:
- **185 GREENWICH STREET** — 10.75 / max(10,10,10)=10.75? (builtfar exceeds 10) → **~108%**
- **1111 AVENUE OF THE AMER** — 25.55 / max(15,10,15)=15 → **~170%**
- **427 10 AVENUE** — 36.48 / max(10,10,10)=10 → **~365%**
- **527 WEST 34 STREET** — 32.96 / 10 → **~330%**
- **One Vanderbilt (51 E 42 ST)** — 38.10 / 15 → **~254%**

> Interpretation caution: This “utilization” metric is a **rough proxy**. In Manhattan, bonuses, special districts, transfers (TDR), and mapped zoning nuances mean built FAR can exceed the simple district FAR fields in PLUTO. So “over 100%” here often means “more complex zoning/bonuses than captured by max(commfar,residfar,facilfar).”
