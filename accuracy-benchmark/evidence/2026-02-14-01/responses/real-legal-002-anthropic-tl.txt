## 1. CHARGE PROGRESSION ANALYSIS

### Original Charges (chargeHistory)
Both charges filed via Information on August 10, 2016:
- **Charge 1:** Battery by Prisoners, ¬ß940.20(1), Felony H
- **Charge 2:** Disorderly Conduct, ¬ß947.01(1), Misdemeanor B

### Final Charges with Enhancers
**No charges were added or dropped**, but significant **modifiers were applied**:

**Charge 1 - Battery by Prisoners:**
- **Modifier 1:** ¬ß939.62(1)(b) - **Repeater** (felony-level enhancer)
- **Modifier 2:** ¬ß939.63(1)(b) - **Use of a Dangerous Weapon**
- Final Disposition: **Found Guilty at Jury Trial** (December 4, 2019)

**Charge 2 - Disorderly Conduct:**
- **Modifier 1:** ¬ß939.62(1)(a) - **Repeater** (misdemeanor-level enhancer)
- **Note:** Judgment indicates "With use of a dangerous weapon enhancer dismissed"
- Final Disposition: **Found Guilty at Jury Trial** (December 4, 2019)

**Key Findings:** The prosecution successfully added repeater enhancements to both charges. The dangerous weapon enhancer was proven for Charge 1 but dismissed for Charge 2, suggesting strategic charging and/or evidentiary limitations.

## CONCLUSION

This case represents a **decade-long criminal justice odyssey** marked by:
- Extreme pre-trial delay (3+ years after arraignment)
- Persistent defense advocacy on speedy trial grounds
- Ultimate vindication at the appellate level
- Incarceration throughout proceedings on separate sentence
- Multi-tier appellate review reaching Wisconsin Supreme Court

**The defendant's speedy trial rights were ultimately vindicated after 10 years of litigation, despite two denials by the circuit court.** The case demonstrates both the protections afforded by the Sixth Amendment and the extraordinary time required to enforce those protections through the appellate process.

**Final Status:** Case dismissed per Court of Appeals mandate, affirmed by Supreme Court. All charges vacated. Case closed October 2, 2025.
