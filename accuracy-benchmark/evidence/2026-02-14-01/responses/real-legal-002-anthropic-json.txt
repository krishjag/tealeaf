# Comprehensive Analysis: State of Wisconsin v. Luis A Ramirez #244212

## 1. CHARGE PROGRESSION ANALYSIS

**Original vs. Final Charges:**
The charges remained **consistent** from complaint through conviction:

| Charge | Offense | Statute | Severity |
|--------|---------|---------|----------|
| Count 1 | Battery by Prisoners | 940.20(1) | Felony H |
| Count 2 | Disorderly Conduct | 947.01(1) | Misd. B |

**Charge Modifiers (Enhancers):**

**Count 1 - Battery by Prisoners:**
- **Repeater Enhancement**: ¬ß939.62(1)(b) - Increases penalties for defendants with prior felony convictions
- **Dangerous Weapon Enhancement**: ¬ß939.63(1)(b) - Increases penalties when dangerous weapon used

**Count 2 - Disorderly Conduct:**
- **Repeater Enhancement**: ¬ß939.62(1)(a) - Misdemeanor-level repeater
- **Note**: Dangerous weapon enhancer was **dismissed** at conviction (per disposition description: "With use of a dangerous weapon enhancer dismissed")

**Key Finding**: The weapon used (a pencil, per exhibit evidence) qualified as a dangerous weapon for the battery charge but was dismissed for the disorderly conduct charge.
