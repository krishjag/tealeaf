## Summary Insights

1. **High cholesterol, high blood pressure, and obesity** dominate as the most prevalent health outcomes across all counties
2. **Sharp urban-rural divide**: Ferry County (smallest, most rural) consistently shows the worst health outcomes
3. **King County** (largest, most urban) generally shows the healthiest metrics, particularly for obesity, diabetes, and smoking
4. **Physical inactivity** shows the largest behavioral disparity, potentially linked to rural vs. urban infrastructure differences
5. **Ferry County's health crisis**: This small rural county shows systemic health challenges across multiple domains, warranting targeted intervention
