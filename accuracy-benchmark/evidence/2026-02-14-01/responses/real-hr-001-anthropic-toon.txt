## 5. Total Separations Verification

✅ **Verified**: Total Separations = Quits + Layoffs & Discharges + Other Separations

Sample checks:
- **Total nonfarm, January**: 3,256 + 1,674 + 342 = 5,272 ✓
- **Manufacturing, March**: 184 + 106 + 21 = 311 ✓
- **Retail trade, December**: 515 + 135 + 14 = 664 ✓

The formula holds across all industries and months within rounding.

## 7. Private Sector vs Government Comparison

| Metric | Total Private | Government | Winner |
|--------|---------------|------------|--------|
| **Average Hire Rate** | 3.68% | 1.45% | Private (2.5x higher) |
| **Average Quit Rate** | 2.21% | 0.78% | Private (2.8x higher) |
| **Average Layoff Rate** | 1.23% | 0.33% | Private (3.7x higher) |

**Key Findings:**
- **Private sector has substantially higher turnover** across all metrics
- Government shows much more employment stability with lower hiring, quitting, and layoff rates
- Private sector's hire rate is 2.5x higher, reflecting faster labor market churn
- Government's low quit rate (0.78%) suggests strong job retention and employee satisfaction with public sector positions
- Private sector's layoff rate is nearly 4x higher than government, indicating more vulnerability to economic fluctuations
