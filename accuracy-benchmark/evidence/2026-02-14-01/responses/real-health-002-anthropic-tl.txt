### Cholesterol Screening Rate (CHOLSCREEN)
**Winner: Garfield County - 86.4%**

- King: 85.6% (3rd)

**Key Finding:** Smaller counties excel at cholesterol screening, while larger urban counties lead in dental care access.

## 6. Population-Weighted Obesity Rate

**Statewide average (weighted by adult population): 29.70%**

| County | Obesity Rate | Weight in Average |
|--------|-------------|-------------------|
| King | 22.6% | 45.9% of total adults |
| Pierce | 36.9% | 17.9% of total adults |
| Spokane | 34.7% | 10.8% of total adults |
| Clark | 35.3% | 10.1% of total adults |
| Other 6 counties | Various | 15.3% of total adults |

**Key Finding:** Despite King County having the lowest obesity rate (22.6%), the statewide average is pulled up to 29.70% by higher rates in other counties, particularly Ferry (40.2%), Yakima (39.7%), and Pierce (36.9%).

- **High cholesterol** affects more than 1 in 3 adults statewide

---

## Key Insights:

1. **Health Equity Gap:** Ferry and Garfield counties show dramatically worse health outcomes across nearly all measures compared to King County
2. **Preventive Care Success:** Cholesterol screening rates exceed 79% in all counties, indicating strong preventive health infrastructure
3. **Sleep Crisis:** Short sleep duration affects 29-37% across all counties - a consistent statewide issue
4. **Obesity Challenge:** Weighted average of 29.70% indicates obesity affects nearly 1 in 3 Washington adults
5. **Disability Burden:** Rural counties show significantly higher disability prevalence, likely related to aging populations and chronic disease burden
