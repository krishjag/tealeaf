## 7. Private Sector vs Government Comparison

| Metric | Total Private | Government | Difference |
|--------|---------------|------------|------------|
| **Average Hire Rate** | 3.68% | 1.45% | +2.23 pp |
| **Average Quit Rate** | 2.21% | 0.80% | +1.41 pp |
| **Average Layoff Rate** | 1.23% | 0.33% | +0.90 pp |

### Key Findings:

1. **Higher Hire Rates in Private Sector:** Private employers hire at 2.5x the rate of government (3.68% vs 1.45%), reflecting greater labor market dynamism

2. **Higher Quit Rates in Private Sector:** Private workers quit at nearly 3x the rate (2.21% vs 0.80%), suggesting:
   - Greater job mobility in private sector
   - Better job security/satisfaction in government
   - Different compensation structures

3. **Higher Layoff Rates in Private Sector:** Private sector layoffs are 3.7x higher (1.23% vs 0.33%), indicating:
   - Greater job security in government positions
   - More exposure to business cycles in private sector
   - Civil service protections in public employment

**Overall Assessment:** The private sector exhibits substantially higher labor market churn across all metrics. Government employment is characterized by lower hiring needs, greater retention (lower quits), and more job security (lower layoffs), consistent with the long-recognized stability of public sector employment.
