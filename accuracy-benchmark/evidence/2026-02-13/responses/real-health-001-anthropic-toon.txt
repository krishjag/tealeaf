## 4. Urban vs Rural Comparison: King County vs Ferry County

| Measure | King (Urban) | Ferry (Rural) | Difference | Rural:Urban Ratio |
|---------|-------------|---------------|------------|-------------------|
| **Smoking (CSMOKING)** | 7.2% (5.8-8.9) | 16.0% (13.5-19.0) | +8.8 pp | 2.2x higher |
| **Obesity (OBESITY)** | 22.6% (20.4-24.9) | 40.2% (33.4-47.9) | +17.6 pp | 1.8x higher |
| **Depression (DEPRESSION)** | 23.5% (21.7-25.3) | 25.0% (21.7-28.8) | +1.5 pp | 1.1x higher |
| **Lack of Health Insurance (ACCESS2)** | 6.8% (5.5-8.4) | 8.5% (6.9-10.4) | +1.7 pp | 1.3x higher |

**Key Finding:** The urban-rural disparity is most dramatic for obesity and smoking, while depression rates and insurance access are relatively similar.

## 5. Widest Health Risk Behavior (RISKBEH) Disparity

**Physical Inactivity (LPA) shows the widest disparity:**

- **Highest:** Yakima County at 27.9% (CI: 24.2-31.7)
- **Lowest:** King County at 13.9% (CI: 12.1-15.8)
- **Disparity:** 14.0 percentage points

**Other RISKBEH disparities:**
- Current Smoking: 8.8 pp (Ferry 16.0% vs King 7.2%)
- Short Sleep Duration: 8.0 pp (Pierce 37.0% vs King 29.0%)
- Binge Drinking: 3.7 pp (Whatcom 17.9% vs Island 14.2%)

## 6. Counties Exceeding 10-County Average by 5+ Percentage Points

**Only Ferry County** meets this threshold, with **4 health outcomes** significantly above average:

| Measure | Ferry Value | 10-County Avg | Difference |
|---------|-------------|---------------|------------|
| **Arthritis** | 38.0% | 26.8% | +11.2 pp |
| **High Blood Pressure** | 41.1% | 32.5% | +8.6 pp |
| **Obesity** | 40.2% | 33.4% | +6.8 pp |
| **High Cholesterol** | 41.2% | 35.8% | +5.4 pp |

**Notable finding:** Ferry County also exceeds the average on 8 other measures (though by less than 5 pp), making it the most health-challenged county in this dataset. Yakima County comes in second with 2 measures exceeding by 5+ pp (Obesity +6.3 pp, All Teeth Lost +6.5 pp).

---

## Summary Insights

1. **High cholesterol** is the most prevalent health outcome statewide, ranking in the top 3 for 9 of 10 counties
2. **Ferry County** (smallest, most rural) consistently shows the worst health outcomes across multiple categories
3. **King County** (largest, most urban) generally shows the best health indicators
4. **Physical inactivity** shows the greatest geographic disparity, nearly double in rural Yakima vs urban King
5. A clear **urban-rural health gradient** exists, with rural counties showing substantially higher rates of chronic disease, smoking, and obesity
