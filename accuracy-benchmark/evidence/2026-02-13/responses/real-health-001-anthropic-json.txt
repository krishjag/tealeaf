## 4. Urban vs. Rural Comparison: King County vs. Ferry County

| Measure | King County (Urban) | Ferry County (Rural) | Difference |
|---------|---------------------|---------------------|------------|
| **Smoking (CSMOKING)** | 7.2% (5.8-8.9) | 16.0% (13.5-19.0) | **+8.8 pp** |
| **Obesity** | 22.6% (20.4-24.9) | 40.2% (33.4-47.9) | **+17.6 pp** |
| **Depression** | 23.5% (21.7-25.3) | 25.0% (21.7-28.8) | **+1.5 pp** |
| **Lack of Health Insurance** | 6.8% (5.5-8.4) | 8.5% (6.9-10.4) | **+1.7 pp** |

**Key Findings:** Ferry County shows dramatically higher rates of smoking (2.2x higher) and obesity (1.8x higher) compared to King County. Depression rates are relatively similar. Health insurance access disparity is modest but still favors the urban county.

## 5. Widest Health Risk Behavior Disparity

**Physical Inactivity (LPA)** shows the widest disparity:

- **Highest:** Yakima County - 27.9% (CI: 24.2-31.7)
- **Lowest:** King County - 13.9% (CI: 12.1-15.8)
- **Disparity:** **14.0 percentage points**

Other disparities:
- Smoking (CSMOKING): 8.8 pp (16.0% Ferry vs. 7.2% King)
- Sleep Duration (SLEEP): 8.0 pp (37.0% Pierce vs. 29.0% King/Island)
- Binge Drinking (BINGE): 3.9 pp (17.9% Whatcom vs. 14.0% Ferry)

## 6. Counties Exceeding Average by 5+ Percentage Points in Multiple Outcomes

**10-County Averages (Selected):**
- Arthritis: 26.8%
- High Blood Pressure: 32.5%
- Obesity: 33.4%
- Diabetes: 10.7%
- High Cholesterol: 35.8%
- All Teeth Lost: 10.7%

**Ferry County** is the only county with **4 or more** health outcomes exceeding the average by 5+ percentage points:

1. **Arthritis:** 38.0% (avg: 26.8%) - **+11.2 pp**
2. **High Blood Pressure:** 41.1% (avg: 32.5%) - **+8.6 pp**
3. **High Cholesterol:** 41.2% (avg: 35.8%) - **+5.4 pp**
4. **Obesity:** 40.2% (avg: 33.4%) - **+6.8 pp**

**Notable Pattern:** Ferry County, the smallest and most rural county in this dataset (population 7,497), shows the worst health outcomes across multiple measures, suggesting significant rural health disparities. These findings align with the county's higher rates of smoking, physical inactivity, and limited healthcare access compared to urban counties.
