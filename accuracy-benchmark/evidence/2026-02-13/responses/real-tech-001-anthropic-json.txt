## 6. Exploitability vs Impact Scores

Analyzing available scores:

**Average Exploitability Score:** 1.82
- Most scores are 1.8 (LOCAL access) or 3.9 (NETWORK access)

**Average Impact Score:** 4.27
- Range: 1.4 to 5.9

**Impact to Exploitability Ratio:** 2.34:1
- Impact scores are on average **2.34× higher** than exploitability scores
- This indicates these vulnerabilities have significant impact but are harder to exploit (requiring local access)

- **Vendor Advisory:** 0%

**Key Finding:** Every single CVE (100%) has at least one patch reference, indicating all vulnerabilities have been addressed with code fixes. The average number of patch references per CVE is approximately 4-5, corresponding to patches for different stable kernel branches.

---

## Summary Insights

1. **Predominantly Medium Severity:** 70% of vulnerabilities are rated MEDIUM
2. **Local Exploitation Required:** 97.5% require local access, limiting remote attack surface
3. **Memory Safety Issues Dominate:** Use-after-free (CWE-416) is most severe class
4. **Excellent Patch Coverage:** 100% of CVEs have available patches
5. **Long Version Spans:** Some vulnerabilities affect 6+ years of kernel versions
6. **High Impact, Lower Exploitability:** Ratio of 2.34:1 suggests serious consequences if exploited, but exploitation requires local access
