## Key Insights & Summary

1. **Urban-Rural Divide:** Ferry and Garfield counties (smallest, most rural) have 50-90% higher chronic disease and disability burdens than King County (largest, most urban).

2. **Obesity Crisis:** Population-weighted obesity rate of 29.70% indicates nearly 1 in 3 Washington adults are obese, with rural counties showing rates up to 40%.

3. **Preventive Care Gaps:** Despite generally high screening rates (>80% for cholesterol), dental and mammography services show larger disparities, suggesting access barriers in rural areas.

4. **Sleep Deprivation:** Short sleep duration (31.51% average) emerges as the most prevalent health risk behavior across the state.

5. **High Cholesterol Prevalence:** Affecting 35.84% of screened adults on average, cardiovascular risk factors are widespread across all counties.

6. **Successful Screening Programs:** Cholesterol screening rates above 79% in all counties demonstrate strong preventive care infrastructure statewide.
