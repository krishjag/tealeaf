## 6. Population-Weighted Obesity Average

**Across all 10 counties: 29.70%**

### Component Calculation:
- Total adult population: 4,004,711
- Weighted by adult population from each county
- King County (lowest obesity at 22.6%) significantly pulls down the overall average due to its large population
- Rural counties like Ferry (40.2%) and Yakima (39.7%) have much higher rates but smaller populations

## Key Findings Summary:

1. **Rural-Urban Divide**: Rural counties (Ferry, Garfield) show significantly higher chronic disease and disability burdens compared to urban King County
2. **Preventive Care Excellence**: Despite health challenges, rural Garfield County leads in cholesterol screening
3. **Obesity Epidemic**: Nearly 30% of Washington adults are obese on average, with rural counties approaching 40%
4. **Sleep Crisis**: Short sleep duration affects nearly 1 in 3 adults statewide
5. **King County Advantage**: The state's most populous county has the lowest disease burden and disability rates, likely reflecting better healthcare access and socioeconomic factors
