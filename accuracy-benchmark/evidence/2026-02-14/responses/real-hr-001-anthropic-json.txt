## 7. Private Sector vs Government Comparison

| Metric | Total Private | Government | Difference |
|--------|---------------:|------------:|----------:|
| **Avg Hire Rate** | 3.7% | 1.4% | **Private +2.3pp** |
| **Avg Quit Rate** | 2.2% | 0.8% | **Private +1.4pp** |
| **Avg Layoff Rate** | 1.2% | 0.3% | **Private +0.9pp** |

### Key Findings:

- **Private sector has HIGHER rates across all three categories**
- **Hire rate**: Private sector hires at 2.6× the government rate (3.7% vs 1.4%)
- **Quit rate**: Private sector sees 2.8× more voluntary separations (2.2% vs 0.8%)
- **Layoff rate**: Private sector has 4× more layoffs (1.2% vs 0.3%)

**Interpretation**: Government employment shows much greater stability with lower turnover in both directions. The private sector has much more labor market dynamism with higher rates of both hiring and separations.

---

## Summary of Key Insights:

1. **Government has the hardest time filling positions** (2.43 openings per hire)
2. **Food service workers quit most frequently** (4.3% rate, 3.6pp above layoffs)
3. **November 2025 saw the steepest decline in job openings** (-521K)
4. **Private sector labor markets are ~3× more dynamic** than government
5. **Construction has highest layoff rates** but shortest gap between quits and layoffs
