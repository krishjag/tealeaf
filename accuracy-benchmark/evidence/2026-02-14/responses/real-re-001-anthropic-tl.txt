## 7. Residential Properties Analysis

**Properties with residential units (unitsres > 0):** 3 properties

| Property | Units | Residential Area/Unit | Assessed Value/Unit | Total Assessed Value |
|----------|-------|---------------------|-------------------|---------------------|
| **240 1 Avenue** | 8,764 units | 955 sf/unit | $90,358/unit | $791,815,950 |
| **MAIN STREET** (Roosevelt Is.) | 8,018 units | 466 sf/unit | $66,608/unit | $534,046,500 |
| **1 Columbus Circle** | 226 units | 1,955 sf/unit | $3,515,931/unit | $794,395,096 |
| **151 East 58 Street** | 105 units | 2,352 sf/unit | $4,824,746/unit | $506,598,307 |

**Key Insights:**

### Affordable vs. Luxury Housing:
- **Affordable housing** (240 1 Ave, MAIN STREET):
  - Smaller units: 466–955 sf/unit
  - Lower assessed value per unit: $66,608–$90,358
  - High unit counts: 8,000+ units
  - 100% tax exempt (MAIN ST) or zero exemption (240 1 Ave)

- **Luxury condos** (Time Warner Center, One Beacon Court):
  - Much larger units: 1,955–2,352 sf/unit
  - Dramatically higher value: $3.5M–$4.8M per unit
  - Low unit counts: 105–226 units
  - Minimal or partial exemptions

### Luxury Premium:
The luxury condos command **53–72× higher assessed value per unit** than affordable housing, reflecting the extreme stratification of Manhattan residential real estate. Unit sizes are also 2–5× larger in luxury buildings.
