## KEY TAKEAWAYS & SIGNIFICANCE

### 1. **Successful Speedy Trial Claim (Rare Outcome)**
Despite initial denial by circuit court, defendant's persistent litigation on speedy trial grounds ultimately prevailed at appellate levelâ€”demonstrating the potency of constitutional claims even after conviction.

### 2. **Decade-Long Case (2015-2025)**
- Offense to final disposition: **10 years, 4 months**
- Pre-trial delay alone: **3+ years**
- Post-conviction litigation: **5+ years**

### 3. **Incarceration Throughout**
Defendant was already serving another sentence when this incident occurred, remained incarcerated throughout all proceedings, and was housed at three different facilities including Wisconsin's supermax (WSPF).

### 4. **Reversal After Conviction**
The case represents a complete victory for the defense after:
- Guilty verdicts on both counts
- 15-year consecutive sentence imposed
- Initial postconviction motion denied
- Supplemental postconviction motion denied
- **Appellate courts ultimately reversed and ordered dismissal**

### 5. **Systemic Issues Illustrated**
The case highlights challenges in the criminal justice system:
- Managing cases involving already-incarcerated defendants
- Multiple continuances stretching speedy trial rights
- Resource constraints (video appearances, transport logistics)
- Tension between efficient case management and constitutional protections

### 6. **Effective Appellate Advocacy**
Attorney Jennifer Lohr's persistent post-conviction litigation strategy, including multiple extension requests and supplemental briefing after remand, ultimately achieved complete reversalâ€”a testament to thorough appellate practice.
