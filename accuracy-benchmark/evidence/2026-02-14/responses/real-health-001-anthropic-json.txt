## 4. Urban vs Rural Comparison: King County vs Ferry County

| Measure | King (Urban) | Ferry (Rural) | Difference |
|---------|--------------|---------------|------------|
| **Current Smoking** | 7.2% (5.8-8.9) | 16.0% (13.5-19.0) | +8.8 pp |
| **Obesity** | 22.6% (20.4-24.9) | 40.2% (33.4-47.9) | +17.6 pp |
| **Depression** | 23.5% (21.7-25.3) | 25.0% (21.7-28.8) | +1.5 pp |
| **Lack of Health Insurance** | 6.8% (5.5-8.4) | 8.5% (6.9-10.4) | +1.7 pp |

**Key Findings:** Ferry County shows significantly higher rates in smoking (2.2x higher) and obesity (1.8x higher) compared to King County. Depression and insurance access show smaller but notable disparities.

## 5. Widest Health Risk Behavior Disparity

**Physical Inactivity (No leisure-time physical activity)** shows the widest disparity:

- **Highest:** Yakima County - 27.9% (CI: 24.2-31.7)
- **Lowest:** King County - 13.9% (CI: 12.1-15.8)
- **Disparity:** 14.0 percentage points

**Other Risk Behavior Disparities:**
- Current Cigarette Smoking: 8.8 pp (Ferry 16.0% vs King 7.2%)
- Short Sleep Duration: 8.0 pp (Pierce 37.0% vs King 29.0%)
- Binge Drinking: 3.9 pp (Whatcom 17.9% vs Ferry 14.0%)

## 6. Counties Exceeding 10-County Averages

**Only Ferry County** has more than 3 health outcomes exceeding the 10-county average by 5+ percentage points:

**Ferry County exceeds average by 5+ points in:**
1. **Arthritis:** 38.0% vs avg 26.8% (+11.2 pp)
2. **High Blood Pressure:** 41.1% vs avg 32.5% (+8.6 pp)
3. **Obesity:** 40.2% vs avg 33.4% (+6.8 pp)
4. **High Cholesterol:** 41.2% vs avg 35.8% (+5.4 pp)

**Honorable mention - Yakima County** exceeds in 2 measures:
- Obesity: 39.7% vs avg 33.4% (+6.3 pp)
- All Teeth Lost: 17.2% vs avg 10.7% (+6.5 pp)

**Key Insight:** Ferry County, the smallest and most rural county (pop. 7,497), shows consistently elevated chronic disease prevalence across multiple indicators, suggesting significant health disparities in rural Washington communities.
