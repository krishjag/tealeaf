## 5. FAR (Floor Area Ratio) Utilization

FAR measures how much building density is used relative to zoning limits.

### **Most Underbuilt (Development Potential Remaining):**

| Property | Address | FAR Utilization | Type |
|----------|---------|-----------------|------|
| 1 | MAIN STREET, Roosevelt Island | **17.4%** | Affordable Housing |
| 2 | 240 1 AVENUE (Stuyvesant Town) | **51.4%** | Affordable Housing |
| 3 | 1 COLUMBUS CIRCLE | **93.7%** | Mixed Use |

### **Near or Exceeding Zoning Limits:**

| Property | Address | FAR Utilization | Notes |
|----------|---------|-----------------|-------|
| 1 | 427 10 AVENUE | **364.8%** | Built 2019, likely used special permits/bonuses |
| 2 | 527 WEST 34 STREET | **329.6%** | Built 2018, Hudson Yards district |
| 3 | 51 EAST 42 STREET (One Vanderbilt) | **254.0%** | 2017, utilized Grand Central air rights |
| 4 | 9 WEST 57 STREET | **228.1%** | 1971 tower |
| 5 | 1221 AVENUE OF THE AMER | **214.4%** | 1973 tower |

**Key Finding:** Newer buildings (2015-2019) dramatically exceed base FAR through:
- Transfer of development rights (TDR)
- Zoning bonuses (transit improvements, public plazas)
- Special district provisions (Hudson Yards, Grand Central)

Older affordable housing complexes are massively underbuilt, though likely restricted from redevelopment.

### **Key Findings:**

**Luxury vs. Affordable Housing Disparity:**
- Luxury condos worth **72x more per unit** than affordable housing
- Luxury units are **5x larger** (2,352 vs 466 sqft)
- Even adjusting for size, luxury units command **21x higher value per sqft**

**Scale Differences:**
- The 2 affordable complexes house **16,782 units** (94% of all residential units in dataset)
- The 2 luxury properties house only **331 units** (2% of residential units)
- Yet combined value is similar: $1.3B (affordable) vs $1.3B (luxury)

**Geographic Pattern:**
- Luxury residential: Midtown (Columbus Circle, East 58th St)
- Affordable housing: East Village, Roosevelt Island (removed from Manhattan core)

## Summary Insights

1. **Value Concentration:** These 30 properties represent $18.3B in assessed value, yet 38% is tax-exempt
2. **Intensity:** Modern towers achieve 250-365% of base FAR through air rights and bonuses
3. **Inequality:** Luxury housing valued at $2,000/sqft vs $95/sqft for affordable units
4. **Public Assets:** Museums, hospitals, convention centers are underbuilt (17-51% FAR utilization)
5. **Commercial Dominance:** 77% are pure commercial/office use; residential is rare at this value tier
