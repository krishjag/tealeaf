## Summary Findings

1. **Valuation Range:** Properties span $437M to $1.67B, with One World Trade Center exceeding #2 by 80%

2. **Density Leaders:** Modern supertalls (One Vanderbilt, Hudson Yards towers) achieve 36-38x building/lot ratios through vertical construction

3. **Value/SF Premium:** Mixed-use luxury properties command $350-460/sf vs $89-125/sf for affordable housing and institutions

4. **Tax Exemptions:** $5.7B (31%) is tax-exempt, primarily from government ownership, public facilities, and development incentives

5. **FAR Gaming:** Top office towers exceed base zoning by 200-365% through air rights transfers and special district bonuses

6. **Residential Divide:** 105 luxury units assessed at $506M vs 16,782 affordable units at $1.3B total - showcasing Manhattan's housing inequality

7. **Asset Types:** 23 commercial office buildings dominate by count, but mixed-use and institutional properties show highest average values
