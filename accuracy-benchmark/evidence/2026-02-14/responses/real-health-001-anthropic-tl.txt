## 4. Urban vs Rural Comparison: King County vs Ferry County

| Measure | King County (Urban) | Ferry County (Rural) | Difference |
|---------|---------------------|----------------------|------------|
| **Smoking** | 7.2% (5.8-8.9) | 16.0% (13.5-19.0) | +8.8 pts (122% higher) |
| **Obesity** | 22.6% (20.4-24.9) | 40.2% (33.4-47.9) | +17.6 pts (78% higher) |
| **Depression** | 23.5% (21.7-25.3) | 25.0% (21.7-28.8) | +1.5 pts (6% higher) |
| **Lack of Insurance** | 6.8% (5.5-8.4) | 8.5% (6.9-10.4) | +1.7 pts (25% higher) |

**Key Finding:** Ferry County shows dramatically worse outcomes for behavioral risk factors (smoking, obesity) but only marginally worse for depression and insurance access. The most striking disparity is in obesity (78% higher) and smoking (122% higher).

## Key Insights

1. **High cholesterol** is the most prevalent health outcome across nearly all counties (9/10 in top 3)
2. **Rural-urban divide is stark:** Ferry County (smallest, most rural) has the worst rates in 8 of 12 health outcomes
3. **King County** (most urban, highest population) consistently has the best health outcomes
4. **Physical inactivity** shows the greatest geographic inequality among behavioral risks
5. **Ferry County is a major outlier**, exceeding state averages dramatically across multiple chronic conditions
